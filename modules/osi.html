1. OSI & TCP/IP Models

To standardize network communication, engineers developed layered models. These models act as a universal blueprint, breaking down the complex process of sending data into smaller, manageable parts called layers. Each layer has a specific job and only talks to the layers directly above and below it.

The Two Key Models

The OSI (Open Systems Interconnection) Model is a 7-layer conceptual framework. The more practical TCP/IP Model has 4 layers and is the foundation of the modern internet. Hover over or focus on a layer to learn its function.
