2. Ethernet & VLANs

Ethernet is the dominant technology for wired Local Area Networks (LANs). It defines how devices format data (into Frames) and how they transmit it over a shared medium. Central to Ethernet are switches, which learn the physical MAC address of each connected device and forward frames only to the intended destination port, unlike hubs which broadcast to all ports.

Virtual LANs (VLANs)

A VLAN allows you to segment a physical LAN into multiple, logically separate broadcast domains. Devices in one VLAN are isolated and cannot communicate directly with devices in another VLAN, even if plugged into the same switch. This enhances security and improves network performance by containing broadcast traffic.
